## The Problem This Tool Solves

Every year, millions of high school students make one of the most consequential financial decisions of their lives — choosing a college and a major — with almost no concrete data about what that decision will cost or earn them. They know tuition is expensive. They don't know whether a $45,000 loan is manageable or catastrophic for the career they're considering, or how living in a rural versus urban area changes their take-home pay.

CareerIQ was built to make that calculation visible, interactive, and accessible — at no cost, with no login, and in a format any teacher can embed in a school website or LMS in under five minutes.

## What CareerIQ Is

CareerIQ is a free, interactive financial literacy tool for 9th–12th grade students. It consists of six integrated tools:

| Tool | What Students Do |
|---|---|
| **Predict Income** | Estimate starting salary by college, major, job title, location, and experience level |
| **Loan Estimator** | See a full breakdown of cost of attendance, financial aid by income bracket, and projected 4-year loan burden |
| **Compare Schools** | Side-by-side salary and 30-year lifetime earnings for any two U.S. colleges |
| **Loan ROI** | Assess whether estimated salary can cover loan payments and living expenses — produces a Comfortable / Manageable / Tight / At Risk verdict |
| **What-If Scenarios** | All 12 location × experience salary combinations displayed simultaneously |
| **Export Report** | Printable one-page PDF summary for use in counseling sessions or parent meetings |

All salary data is sourced from the **BLS National Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OES)** survey. College cost and financial aid data is sourced from the **College Scorecard API** and **IPEDS SFA Survey**. Federal loan rates reflect **2024–25 actuals** from Federal Student Aid. The tool covers **1,805 U.S. colleges and universities**.

## Alignment with W!SE Financial Literacy Certification

CareerIQ directly supports instruction in the following W!SE curriculum topic areas:

**Income & Earnings**
- Sources of income (wages, career paths, entrepreneurship)
- How college and major choice affect lifetime earning potential
- Geographic and experience-based salary variation (opportunity cost of location)

**Loans & Debt**
- How student loans are structured (federal direct loans, PLUS loans, blended rates)
- Compound interest applied to a real-world loan scenario
- Federal loan caps by year ($5,500 / $6,500 / $7,500 / $7,500 for dependent undergraduates)
- 10-year standard repayment mechanics; reference to income-driven repayment alternatives

**Budgeting & Discretionary Income**
- Monthly budget modeling: gross income → taxes → loan payment → living expenses → disposable income
- Definition and application of discretionary income in a post-graduation context
- Debt-to-income thresholds and their practical meaning

**Decision-Making & Opportunity Cost**
- Side-by-side school comparison quantifying the financial cost of choosing one path over another
- What-If Scenarios grid demonstrating how location and career progression interact

CareerIQ is designed as a **supplement** to full W!SE curriculum instruction — not a replacement. Topics tested on the Financial Literacy Certification Test that fall outside CareerIQ's scope (banking products, credit scores, insurance, taxes at the full-curriculum level) remain the domain of direct instruction and the W!SE resources directory.

## Why It Works in a Classroom

- **No login required.** Students open the tool and start immediately.
- **No student data collected.** Full FERPA/COPPA compatibility — nothing passes between the tool and the host site.
- **Embeds anywhere.** Works in any LMS, school website, WordPress, Squarespace, or Webflow via a single iFrame snippet. Full embed documentation included.
- **Printable Export Report.** Designed specifically for counseling sessions — students generate a one-page summary of their college, major, salary estimate, and loan burden that can be reviewed with a teacher, counselor, or parent.
- **Mobile responsive.** Works on school Chromebooks and personal phones down to 320px screen width.

---

## Data Integrity & Limitations

All figures are **gross income estimates for educational purposes only**. Salary estimates reflect published national market data; actual earnings vary by employer, negotiation, and economic conditions. Tax calculations use a national average effective rate — actual take-home varies by state. Federal loan rate figures are cited by academic year and updated annually. The tool's footer and disclaimer text makes these limitations explicit at every relevant point.
